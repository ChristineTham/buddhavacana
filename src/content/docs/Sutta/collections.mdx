The Five Collections acting as fuel (`paÃ±cupÄdÄnakkhandhÄ`) breaks our sense of identity down into components, and show that the sum total of who we believe we are are nothing more than a impermanent and ever shifting collection of processed phenomena experienced through our senses. It is clearly a refinement of the dependent origination framework. The Buddha uses it to disprove prevailing Brahmanical and Jain ideas of an eternal self that can be "purified" in order to attain "enlightenment".
