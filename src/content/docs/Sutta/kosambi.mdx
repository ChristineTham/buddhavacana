[12S2/1.7.8 Kosambisutta](https://tipitaka2500.github.io/tipitaka/12S2/1/1.7/1.7.8.html)

This is an important sutta that shows insight into the dhamma (phenomenal nature of experience) does not necessarily result in liberation - further work needs to be done in accordance to the Buddha's teaching (reflective thinking, memory reconsolidation, etc.). Liberation is based on direct experience and personal knowledge, not by

* faith,
* inclination,
* tradition,
* reasoned argument,
* approval of a view.

It is interesting that this sutta does not include the presence of the Buddha, so unlikely to be a teaching originated by him but an explanation created by a disciple.

This may be an indication of the declining efficacy of the Buddha's teaching after the Buddha's death. It is strongly implied that during the Buddha's lifetime, all of his 1250 disciples with the exception of Ä€nanda were awakened. This shows that after his death, some disciples only gained the insight into the dhamma but were unable to reach liberation.
