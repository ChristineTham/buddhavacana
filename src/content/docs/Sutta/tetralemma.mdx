This is a group of suttas relating to the infamous "tetralemma" which has puzzled Buddhists for centuries. As can be seen from the explanation, the key to understanding the tetralemma is to realise the concept of a `TathÄgata` (someone who is liberated from the [five collections](/buddhavacana/sutta/collections)) cannot be associated with the concept of `paraá¹ƒ maraá¹‡Ä` (after death) since the concept of "existence" and "death" are intrinsically linked with the five collections.

Note that `hoti` can have multiple meanings including "become/exist/stay/remain". I have chosen to translate it as "remain" in the tetralemma because that makes the most sense.
