This is a group of suttas relating to the infamous "tetralemma" which has puzzled Buddhists for centuries. As can be seen from the explanation, the key to understanding the tetralemma is to realise the concept of a `Tathāgata` (someone who is liberated from the [five collections](/buddhavacana/sutta/collections)) cannot be associated with the concept of `paraṃ maraṇā` (after death) since the concept of "existence" and "death" are intrinsically linked with the five collections (and the mistaken concept of a permanent, eternal "self").

"Death" implies the cessation of "existence", but someone who is liberated understands that existence implies persistence of a stable entity, and since our sense of "self" (comprising of the five collections) is impermanent and ephemeral, "existence" is non applicable, and therefore neither is "death".

Note that `hoti` can have multiple meanings including "become/exist/stay/remain". I have chosen to translate it as "remain" in the tetralemma because that makes the most sense.

## [13S4/10.1.1 Khemāsutta](https://tipitaka2500.github.io/tipitaka/13S4/10/10.1/10.1.1.html)

<Tabs syncKey="paliquote">
<TabItem label="My Translation">
1749\. At one time the Bhagavā was staying at Sāvatthī, in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. Now, at that time, the bhikkhunī Khemā, while wandering in the Kosalas, had arrived at Toraṇavatthu, between Sāvatthī and Sāketa, for a stay. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala, while travelling from Sāketa to Sāvatthī, stopped for a night at Toraṇavatthu, between Sāketa and Sāvatthī. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala addressed a certain man thus: "Come, good man, find out if there is any renunciant or brahmin of such a nature in Toraṇavatthu whom I might visit today."
</TabItem>

<TabItem label="Pāḷi (Roman IAST)">
1749\. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme. Tena kho pana samayena khemā bhikkhunī kosalesu cārikaṃ caramānā antarā ca sāvatthiṃ antarā ca sāketaṃ toraṇavatthusmiṃ vāsaṃ upagatā hoti. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo sāketā sāvatthiṃ gacchanto, antarā ca sāketaṃ antarā ca sāvatthiṃ toraṇavatthusmiṃ ekarattivāsaṃ upagacchi. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo aññataraṃ purisaṃ āmantesi—  “ehi tvaṃ, ambho purisa, toraṇavatthusmiṃ tathārūpaṃ samaṇaṃ vā brāhmaṇaṃ vā jāna yamahaṃ ajja payirupāseyyan”ti.
</TabItem>

<TabItem label="Pāḷi (Brahmi)">
1749\. 𑀏𑀓𑀁 𑀲𑀫𑀬𑀁 𑀪𑀕𑀯𑀸 𑀲𑀸𑀯𑀢𑁆𑀣𑀺𑀬𑀁 𑀯𑀺𑀳𑀭𑀢𑀺 𑀚𑁂𑀢𑀯𑀦𑁂 𑀅𑀦𑀸𑀣𑀧𑀺𑀡𑁆𑀟𑀺𑀓𑀲𑁆𑀲 𑀆𑀭𑀸𑀫𑁂. 𑀢𑁂𑀦 𑀔𑁄 𑀧𑀦 𑀲𑀫𑀬𑁂𑀦 𑀔𑁂𑀫𑀸 𑀪𑀺𑀓𑁆𑀔𑀼𑀦𑀻 𑀓𑁄𑀲𑀮𑁂𑀲𑀼 𑀘𑀸𑀭𑀺𑀓𑀁 𑀘𑀭𑀫𑀸𑀦𑀸 𑀅𑀦𑁆𑀢𑀭𑀸 𑀘 𑀲𑀸𑀯𑀢𑁆𑀣𑀺𑀁 𑀅𑀦𑁆𑀢𑀭𑀸 𑀘 𑀲𑀸𑀓𑁂𑀢𑀁 𑀢𑁄𑀭𑀡𑀯𑀢𑁆𑀣𑀼𑀲𑁆𑀫𑀺𑀁 𑀯𑀸𑀲𑀁 𑀉𑀧𑀕𑀢𑀸 𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺. 𑀅𑀣 𑀔𑁄 𑀭𑀸𑀚𑀸 𑀧𑀲𑁂𑀦𑀤𑀺 𑀓𑁄𑀲𑀮𑁄 𑀲𑀸𑀓𑁂𑀢𑀸 𑀲𑀸𑀯𑀢𑁆𑀣𑀺𑀁 𑀕𑀘𑁆𑀙𑀦𑁆𑀢𑁄, 𑀅𑀦𑁆𑀢𑀭𑀸 𑀘 𑀲𑀸𑀓𑁂𑀢𑀁 𑀅𑀦𑁆𑀢𑀭𑀸 𑀘 𑀲𑀸𑀯𑀢𑁆𑀣𑀺𑀁 𑀢𑁄𑀭𑀡𑀯𑀢𑁆𑀣𑀼𑀲𑁆𑀫𑀺𑀁 𑀏𑀓𑀭𑀢𑁆𑀢𑀺𑀯𑀸𑀲𑀁 𑀉𑀧𑀕𑀘𑁆𑀙𑀺. 𑀅𑀣 𑀔𑁄 𑀭𑀸𑀚𑀸 𑀧𑀲𑁂𑀦𑀤𑀺 𑀓𑁄𑀲𑀮𑁄 𑀅𑀜𑁆𑀜𑀢𑀭𑀁 𑀧𑀼𑀭𑀺𑀲𑀁 𑀆𑀫𑀦𑁆𑀢𑁂𑀲𑀺—  “𑀏𑀳𑀺 𑀢𑁆𑀯𑀁, 𑀅𑀫𑁆𑀪𑁄 𑀧𑀼𑀭𑀺𑀲, 𑀢𑁄𑀭𑀡𑀯𑀢𑁆𑀣𑀼𑀲𑁆𑀫𑀺𑀁 𑀢𑀣𑀸𑀭𑀽𑀧𑀁 𑀲𑀫𑀡𑀁 𑀯𑀸 𑀩𑁆𑀭𑀸𑀳𑁆𑀫𑀡𑀁 𑀯𑀸 𑀚𑀸𑀦 𑀬𑀫𑀳𑀁 𑀅𑀚𑁆𑀚 𑀧𑀬𑀺𑀭𑀼𑀧𑀸𑀲𑁂𑀬𑁆𑀬𑀦𑁆”𑀢𑀺.
</TabItem>
</Tabs>

The king was told there were no suitable renunciants or brahmins, but the bhikkhunī Khemā, who was a disciple of the Buddha, had a good reputation. So the king decided to pay homage to her.

<Tabs syncKey="paliquote">
<TabItem label="My Translation">
1752\. Then King Pasenadi of Kosala approached the bhikkhunī Khemā. Having approached the bhikkhunī Khemā and paid homage, he sat down to one side. Seated to one side, King Pasenadi of Kosala said this to the bhikkhunī Khemā:

> “Ayye, does a Tathāgata remain after death?”

> “Mahārāja, this has been undeclared by the Bhagavā: ‘a Tathāgata remains after death.’”

> “But Ayye, does a Tathāgata not remain after death?”

> “Mahārāja, this too has been undeclared by the Bhagavā: ‘a Tathāgata does not remain after death.’”

> “Ayye, does a Tathāgata both remain and not remain after death?”

> “Mahārāja, this too has been undeclared by the Bhagavā: ‘a Tathāgata both remains and does not remain after death.’”

> “But Ayye, does a Tathāgata neither remain nor not remain after death?”

> “Mahārāja, this too has been undeclared by the Bhagavā: ‘a Tathāgata neither remains nor does not remain after death.’”
</TabItem>

<TabItem label="Pāḷi (Roman IAST)">
1752\. Atha kho rājā pasenadi kosalo yena khemā bhikkhunī tenupasaṅkami; upasaṅkamitvā khemaṃ bhikkhuniṃ abhivādetvā ekamantaṃ nisīdi. Ekamantaṃ nisinno kho rājā pasenadi kosalo khemaṃ bhikkhuniṃ etadavoca—  “kiṃ nu kho, ayye, hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā”ti? “Abyākataṃ kho etaṃ, mahārāja, bhagavatā—  ‘hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’”ti. “Kiṃ panayye, na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā”ti? “Etampi kho, mahārāja, abyākataṃ bhagavatā—  ‘na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’”ti. “Kiṃ nu kho, ayye, hoti ca na ca hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā”ti? “Abyākataṃ kho etaṃ, mahārāja, bhagavatā—  ‘hoti ca na ca hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’”ti. “Kiṃ panayye, neva hoti na na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā”ti. “Etampi kho, mahārāja, abyākataṃ bhagavatā—  ‘neva hoti na na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’”ti.
</TabItem>

1752\. 𑀅𑀣 𑀔𑁄 𑀭𑀸𑀚𑀸 𑀧𑀲𑁂𑀦𑀤𑀺 𑀓𑁄𑀲𑀮𑁄 𑀬𑁂𑀦 𑀔𑁂𑀫𑀸 𑀪𑀺𑀓𑁆𑀔𑀼𑀦𑀻 𑀢𑁂𑀦𑀼𑀧𑀲𑀗𑁆𑀓𑀫𑀺; 𑀉𑀧𑀲𑀗𑁆𑀓𑀫𑀺𑀢𑁆𑀯𑀸 𑀔𑁂𑀫𑀁 𑀪𑀺𑀓𑁆𑀔𑀼𑀦𑀺𑀁 𑀅𑀪𑀺𑀯𑀸𑀤𑁂𑀢𑁆𑀯𑀸 𑀏𑀓𑀫𑀦𑁆𑀢𑀁 𑀦𑀺𑀲𑀻𑀤𑀺. 𑀏𑀓𑀫𑀦𑁆𑀢𑀁 𑀦𑀺𑀲𑀺𑀦𑁆𑀦𑁄 𑀔𑁄 𑀭𑀸𑀚𑀸 𑀧𑀲𑁂𑀦𑀤𑀺 𑀓𑁄𑀲𑀮𑁄 𑀔𑁂𑀫𑀁 𑀪𑀺𑀓𑁆𑀔𑀼𑀦𑀺𑀁 𑀏𑀢𑀤𑀯𑁄𑀘—  “𑀓𑀺𑀁 𑀦𑀼 𑀔𑁄, 𑀅𑀬𑁆𑀬𑁂, 𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺 𑀢𑀣𑀸𑀕𑀢𑁄 𑀧𑀭𑀁 𑀫𑀭𑀡𑀸”𑀢𑀺? “𑀅𑀩𑁆𑀬𑀸𑀓𑀢𑀁 𑀔𑁄 𑀏𑀢𑀁, 𑀫𑀳𑀸𑀭𑀸𑀚, 𑀪𑀕𑀯𑀢𑀸—  ‘𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺 𑀢𑀣𑀸𑀕𑀢𑁄 𑀧𑀭𑀁 𑀫𑀭𑀡𑀸’”𑀢𑀺. “𑀓𑀺𑀁 𑀧𑀦𑀬𑁆𑀬𑁂, 𑀦 𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺 𑀢𑀣𑀸𑀕𑀢𑁄 𑀧𑀭𑀁 𑀫𑀭𑀡𑀸”𑀢𑀺? “𑀏𑀢𑀫𑁆𑀧𑀺 𑀔𑁄, 𑀫𑀳𑀸𑀭𑀸𑀚, 𑀅𑀩𑁆𑀬𑀸𑀓𑀢𑀁 𑀪𑀕𑀯𑀢𑀸—  ‘𑀦 𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺 𑀢𑀣𑀸𑀕𑀢𑁄 𑀧𑀭𑀁 𑀫𑀭𑀡𑀸’”𑀢𑀺. “𑀓𑀺𑀁 𑀦𑀼 𑀔𑁄, 𑀅𑀬𑁆𑀬𑁂, 𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺 𑀘 𑀦 𑀘 𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺 𑀢𑀣𑀸𑀕𑀢𑁄 𑀧𑀭𑀁 𑀫𑀭𑀡𑀸”𑀢𑀺? “𑀅𑀩𑁆𑀬𑀸𑀓𑀢𑀁 𑀔𑁄 𑀏𑀢𑀁, 𑀫𑀳𑀸𑀭𑀸𑀚, 𑀪𑀕𑀯𑀢𑀸—  ‘𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺 𑀘 𑀦 𑀘 𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺 𑀢𑀣𑀸𑀕𑀢𑁄 𑀧𑀭𑀁 𑀫𑀭𑀡𑀸’”𑀢𑀺. “𑀓𑀺𑀁 𑀧𑀦𑀬𑁆𑀬𑁂, 𑀦𑁂𑀯 𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺 𑀦 𑀦 𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺 𑀢𑀣𑀸𑀕𑀢𑁄 𑀧𑀭𑀁 𑀫𑀭𑀡𑀸”𑀢𑀺. “𑀏𑀢𑀫𑁆𑀧𑀺 𑀔𑁄, 𑀫𑀳𑀸𑀭𑀸𑀚, 𑀅𑀩𑁆𑀬𑀸𑀓𑀢𑀁 𑀪𑀕𑀯𑀢𑀸—  ‘𑀦𑁂𑀯 𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺 𑀦 𑀦 𑀳𑁄𑀢𑀺 𑀢𑀣𑀸𑀕𑀢𑁄 𑀧𑀭𑀁 𑀫𑀭𑀡𑀸’”𑀢𑀺.

Naturally puzzled, the king sought an explanation.

<Tabs syncKey="paliquote">
<TabItem label="My Translation">
> 1754\. “Then, Mahārāja, I shall question you about this in return. You should answer as you see fit. What do you think, Mahārāja, do you have any accountant, auditor, or enumerator who is able to count the sand of the Ganges — saying, ‘There are so many grains of sand,’ or ‘so many hundreds of grains of sand,’ or ‘so many thousands of grains of sand,’ or ‘so many hundreds of thousands of grains of sand’?”

> “No, Ayye.”

> “But do you have any accountant, auditor, or enumerator who is able to count the water in the great ocean — saying, ‘There are so many measures of water,’ or ‘so many hundreds of measures of water,’ or ‘so many thousands of measures of water,’ or ‘so many hundreds of thousands of measures of water’?”

> “No, Ayye.”

> “For what reason?”

> “Reverend lady, the ocean is deep, immeasurable, and unfathomable.”

> “Even so, Mahārāja, that form by which one would define a Tathāgata — that `rūpaṃ` (form) has been abandoned by the Tathāgata, its root cut off, made like a palm stump, annihilated, and is no longer subject to future arising. The Tathāgata, Mahārāja, is freed from reckoning by form. He is deep, immeasurable, unfathomable — just like the great ocean.
>
> * It does not apply to say ‘the Tathāgata remains after death’;
> * it does not apply to say ‘the Tathāgata does not remain after death’;
> * it does not apply to say ‘the Tathāgata both remains and does not remain after death’;
> * it does not apply to say ‘the Tathāgata neither remains nor does not remain after death’.”
</TabItem>

1754\. “Tena hi, mahārāja, taññevettha paṭipucchissāmi. Yathā te khameyya tathā naṃ byākareyyāsi. Taṃ kiṃ maññasi, mahārāja, atthi te koci gaṇako vā muddiko vā saṅkhāyako vā yo pahoti gaṅgāya vālukaṃ gaṇetuṃ—  ettakā vālukā iti vā, ettakāni vālukasatāni iti vā, ettakāni vālukasahassāni iti vā, ettakāni vālukasatasahassāni iti vā”ti? “No hetaṃ, ayye”. “Atthi pana te koci gaṇako vā muddiko vā saṅkhāyako vā yo pahoti mahāsamudde udakaṃ gaṇetuṃ—  ettakāni udakāḷhakāni iti vā, ettakāni udakāḷhakasatāni iti vā, ettakāni udakāḷhakasahassāni iti vā, ettakāni udakāḷhakasatasahassāni iti vā”ti? “No hetaṃ, ayye”. “Taṃ kissa hetu”? “Mahāyye, samuddo gambhīro appameyyo duppariyogāho”ti. “Evameva kho, mahārāja, yena rūpena tathāgataṃ paññāpayamāno paññāpeyya taṃ rūpaṃ tathāgatassa pahīnaṃ ucchinnamūlaṃ tālāvatthukataṃ anabhāvaṃkataṃ āyatiṃ anuppādadhammaṃ. Rūpasaṅkhāyavimutto kho, mahārāja, tathāgato gambhīro appameyyo duppariyogāho—  seyyathāpi mahāsamuddo. ‘Hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’tipi na upeti, ‘na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’tipi na upeti, ‘hoti ca na ca hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’tipi na upeti, ‘neva hoti na na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’tipi na upeti.

## [13S4/10.1.2 Anurādhasutta](https://tipitaka2500.github.io/tipitaka/13S4/10/10.1/10.1.2.html)

<Tabs syncKey="paliquote">
<TabItem label="My Translation">
1763\. At one time the Bhagavā was staying at Vesālī, in the Great Wood, at the Gabled Hall. Now, at that time, āyasmā Anurādha was staying in a forest hut not far from the Bhagavā. Then many wandering renunciants of other sects approached āyasmā Anurādha. Having approached, they exchanged cordial greetings with āyasmā Anurādha. Having concluded their cordial and polite talk, they sat down to one side. Seated to one side, those wandering renunciants of other sects said this to āyasmā Anurādha:

> “Friend Anurādha, that Tathāgata, the supreme person, the ultimate person, who has attained the ultimate attainment, does he declare the Tathāgata in these four ways:
>
> * ‘a Tathāgata remains after death,’ or
> * ‘a Tathāgata does not remain after death,’ or
> * ‘a Tathāgata both remains and does not remain after death,’ or
> * ‘a Tathāgata neither remains nor does not remain after death’?”

> “Friend, that Tathāgata, the supreme person, the ultimate person, who has attained the ultimate attainment, declares the Tathāgata apart from these four ways:
>
> * ‘a Tathāgata remains after death,’ or
> * ‘a Tathāgata does not remain after death,’ or
> * ‘a Tathāgata both remains and does not remain after death,’ or
> * ‘a Tathāgata neither remains nor does not remain after death’?”

When this was said, those wandering renunciants of other sects said this to āyasmā Anurādha:

> “This bhikkhu must be new, recently ordained, or else an elder who is foolish and inexperienced.”

Then those wandering renunciants of other sects, having disparaged āyasmā Anurādha with words of being new and words of being foolish, rose from their seats and departed.

1763\. Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā vesāliyaṃ viharati mahāvane kūṭāgārasālāyaṃ. Tena kho pana samayena āyasmā anurādho bhagavato avidūre araññakuṭikāyaṃ viharati. Atha kho sambahulā aññatitthiyā paribbājakā yenāyasmā anurādho tenupasaṅkamiṃsu; upasaṅkamitvā āyasmatā anurādhena saddhiṃ sammodiṃsu. Sammodanīyaṃ kathaṃ sāraṇīyaṃ vītisāretvā ekamantaṃ nisīdiṃsu. Ekamantaṃ nisinnā kho te aññatitthiyā paribbājakā āyasmantaṃ anurādhaṃ etadavocuṃ—  “yo so, āvuso anurādha, tathāgato uttamapuriso paramapuriso paramapattipatto, taṃ tathāgato imesu catūsu ṭhānesu paññāpayamāno paññāpeti—  ‘hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’ti vā, ‘na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’ti vā, ‘hoti ca na ca hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’ti vā, ‘neva hoti na na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’ti vā”ti? “Yo so, āvuso, tathāgato uttamapuriso paramapuriso paramapattipatto, taṃ tathāgato aññatra imehi catūhi ṭhānehi paññāpayamāno paññāpeti—  ‘hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’ti vā, ‘na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’ti vā, ‘hoti ca na ca hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇā’ti vā, neva hoti na na hoti tathāgato paraṃ maraṇāti vā”ti. Evaṃ vutte, te aññatitthiyā paribbājakā āyasmantaṃ anurādhaṃ etadavocuṃ—  “so cāyaṃ bhikkhu navo bhavissati acirapabbajito, thero vā pana bālo abyatto”ti. Atha kho te aññatitthiyā paribbājakā āyasmantaṃ anurādhaṃ navavādena ca bālavādena ca apasādetvā uṭṭhāyāsanā pakkamiṃsu.

Clearly, Anurādha is confused, and believed the solution to the tetralemma is a fifth (unpostulated) state ("apart from these four ways").

He seeks clarification from the Buddha, who teaches a variation of the Second Discourse ([13S3/1.2.1.7 Anattalakkhaṇasutta](https://tipitaka2500.github.io/tipitaka/13S3/1/1.2/1.2.1/1.2.1.7.html)) to him, and then queries him whether the Tathāgata can be regarded as

* any of the [five collections](/buddhavacana/sutta/collections), or
* within the five collections, or
* distinct from the five collections, or
* possessing any of the five collections, 
* without any of the five collections.

Anurādha answers no to all the above questions. The Buddha then continues:

<TabItem label="My Translation">
> 1779\. “What do you think, Anurādha, do you regard form, feeling, apperception, mental constructions, consciousness as a Tathāgata?”

> “No, Bhante.”

> “What do you think, Anurādha, do you regard this as a Tathāgata who is formless, feeling-less, apperception-less, mental-construction-less, consciousness-less?”

> “No, Bhante.”

> “And since, Anurādha, even in this very life, the Tathāgata is not found by you as true and real, is it fitting for you to make that declaration:
>
> > ‘That Tathāgata, the supreme person, the ultimate person, who has attained the ultimate attainment, declares the Tathāgata apart from these four ways:
> > * “a Tathāgata remains after death,” or
> > * “a Tathāgata does not remain after death,” or
> > * “a Tathāgata both remains and does not remain after death,” or
> > * “a Tathāgata neither remains nor does not remain after death”’?”

> “No, Bhante.”

> “Good, good, Anurādha. Formerly, Anurādha, and now, I declare only suffering and the cessation of suffering.”

## [13S4/10.1.3 Paṭhamasāriputtakoṭṭhikasutta](https://tipitaka2500.github.io/tipitaka/13S4/10/10.1/10.1.3.html)

The context is that at one time, āyasmā Sāriputta and āyasmā Mahākoṭṭhiko were staying at Bārāṇasī, in Isipatana, the Deer Park. Then, in the evening, āyasmā Mahākoṭṭhiko, rising from seclusion, approached āyasmā Sāriputta. Having approached, he exchanged cordial greetings with āyasmā Sāriputta. Having concluded their cordial and polite talk, he sat down to one side. Seated to one side, āyasmā Mahākoṭṭhiko asked āyasmā Sāriputta about the tetralemma.

Sāriputta gave a variation of the answer, by pointing out the four statements of the tetralemma are intrinsically linked with the concept of the [five collections](/buddhavacana/sutta/collections).

<Tabs syncKey="paliquote">
<TabItem label="My Translation">
> * 1784\. “‘A Tathāgata remains after death’ — this, friend, pertains to form.
> * ‘A Tathāgata does not remain after death’ — this pertains to form.
> * ‘A Tathāgata both remains and does not remain after death’ — this pertains to form.
> * ‘A Tathāgata neither remains nor does not remain after death’—this pertains to form.

[the tetralemma also pertains to feelings, apperceptions, mental constructions, consciousness]

> This, friend, is the reason, this is the cause, why this has been undeclared by the Bhagavā.”
</TabItem>
